## Part 1 — Outreach scripts

Principles: 60–90 words, founder-to-founder, zero pitch, ask for a research conversation not a demo, offer to share findings. Never recruit with the phrase "AI co-founder" — that's the variable under test; leaking it into outreach contaminates the playback score.

### A. Warm network DM (X / LinkedIn / Discord)

> Hey [name] — I'm researching how founders actually make and track big decisions (not tasks — the bet-the-company kind). I'm doing 20 short conversations with founders this month, 25 minutes, no pitch. You came to mind because [specific reason]. I'll send you the synthesis of everything I learn when it's done. Open to it this week?

### B. Cold email

> **Subject:** how you made your last big call
>
> [Name] — I'm the founder of Dead Star Labs, doing research on how founder-operators make strategic decisions: what you use to think them through, what happens to your assumptions afterward, where the tools fall down.
>
> 25 minutes, purely research — I'm not selling anything on the call. In exchange you get the full write-up of what 20 founders told me.
>
> [Two one-click scheduling slots] — or reply with a better time.

### C. Community post (Indie Hackers / founder Slack / subreddit where self-promo-free research posts are allowed)

> Doing research on founder decision-making — how you make big calls, track assumptions, and whether you ever revisit why you decided something. Looking to talk to ~20 founders, 25 min each, no product pitch on the call, and everyone who participates gets the full synthesis. DM me or grab a slot: [link]

### D. Referral follow-up (send within 24h of each interview)

> Thanks again for today — [one specific thing they said that was genuinely useful]. Two asks: (1) who are 2 founders you know with strong opinions about how they make decisions? An intro or just a name helps. (2) I'll send the synthesis in ~2 weeks.

**Screener (before booking):** currently running a company they own decisions for; makes strategy calls (not only execution); any AI tool usage at least weekly. Disqualify: aspiring founders, employees, agencies pitching services.

## Part 2 — Interview guide (25 min)

Record with permission, or take verbatim notes — the exact words are the deliverable.

### §1 Context — 3 min
- What are you building, and what stage are you at? (team size, revenue-ish, solo vs co-founded)
- What did your week look like? *(baseline: execution-dominated or decision-dominated)*

### §2 Decision discovery — 8 min *(tests A1 + A9; product still unmentioned)*
- Walk me through the last big decision you made for the company. How did you actually make it?
- What did you use to think it through — docs, people, tools? What did that process feel like?
- After you decided: where does the reasoning live now? Could you reconstruct *why* in six months?
- Do you keep track of assumptions you're betting on? Has one ever quietly gone stale on you? What did that cost?
- When did you last change your mind about strategy? What triggered it?
- What's the most expensive decision mistake of the last year — and would better information, better pressure-testing, or better memory have caught it?

**Listen for (mark unprompted mentions):**
- decision-quality / accountability / "no one pushes back on me" pain → **supports A1, A9**
- task-overload / automation pain framed as the real problem → **invalidates A1**
- lost context, forgotten rationale, stale assumptions → memory pain (core wedge)

### §3 Current alternatives — 4 min
- What have you tried for this? (notion/docs, advisors, a co-founder, therapist, nothing)
- Where do ChatGPT/Claude fit in your company decisions today? Where do they fail you?
- What would have to be true for you to trust an AI's input on a bet-the-company call?

### §4 Positioning test — 7 min *(the measured section — read the one-liner verbatim, then shut up)*

**One-liner (hold constant across all 20 interviews):**
> "Zade is an AI co-founder that runs entirely on your own machine. It keeps your assumptions, bets, goals, decisions, and evidence as living objects — it remembers everything, challenges your thinking, and shows you the evidence behind every recommendation."

1. **PLAYBACK (scored):** "In your own words — what is this, and who is it for?"
   - **Accurate** = names (a) strategy/decision operating system with memory/evidence, AND (b) for founders/operators — without reducing it to a chatbot or task manager.
   - **Inaccurate** = "assistant," "chatbot," "task manager," "note app," or can't say.
2. **PHRASE (scored):** "The phrase 'AI co-founder' — does that feel right for what I described? Or does it read as misleading, unserious, or confusing?" *(record their word choice verbatim)*
3. "What would you compare it to?" *(comparison set → A9: assistants/note-takers = bad signal; advisor/board-member/co-founder = good)*
4. "What would you expect it to do in your first week?"
5. "It runs 100% locally — your strategy and data never leave your machine. Does that change anything for you? How much?"

### §5 Commitment test — 3 min *(compliments are worthless; get a cost)*
- "Would you actually use this weekly?" If yes: "I'm putting together a bootstrap group of 5 founders who run their company through it and do a weekly review. Want in?" → **feeds Goal 3 + the activated-user funnel**
- "What would this have to do for [what they said in §2] to be worth paying for? What number feels fair?"
- Score commitment: **0** none · **1** gave email / wants the synthesis · **2** joins weekly bootstrap cohort · **3** states a price they'd pay

### §6 Close — 1 min
- Referral ask (2 names), permission to follow up, promise the synthesis.
